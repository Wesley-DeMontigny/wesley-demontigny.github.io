Wesley DeMontigny

My name is Wesley DeMontigny, and I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Maryland developing probabilistic models for complex biological data. My research centers on statistical phylogenetics and focuses on building principled models that integrate structure, uncertainty, and domain knowledge to extract signal from noisy and incomplete datasets. Across my work, I've applied Bayesian statistics, graphical modeling, and modern machine learning to address problems where standard approaches are insufficient.

While my work is grounded in evolutionary biology, my broader interest lies in designing quantitative models for challenging problems more generally. I am particularly drawn to settings with interesting structure, where careful modeling can lead to meaningful and interpretable insights.
