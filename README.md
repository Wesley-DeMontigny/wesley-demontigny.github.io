Hi! I'm Wesley DeMontigny, a Ph.D. student in biology at the University of Maryland. I work mostly on probabilistic modeling for biological systems. This blog is where I share projects, modeling intuitions, papers I'm thinking about, and other ideas I'm exploring.

## Publications

### Bioinformatics / Data Analysis
- [**The nuclear and mitochondrial genomes of _Amoebophrya_ sp. ex _Karlodinium veneficum_**](https://doi.org/10.1093/g3journal/jkaf030) <br>
  In this study I performed bioinformatic analyses on a novel marine parasite genome, focusing on alternative genetic code usage and apparent heterogeneity of mitochondrial genomes.

### Statistical Modeling
- [**Inferring Gene Presence in Incomplete Data via Phylogenetic Occupancy Modeling**](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.02.27.708499v1) <br>
  In this paper I developed and benchmarked a novel statistical model, utilizing belief networks to construct a joint distribution over latent gene presence.

## Software & Tools

### [Smc-CAT](https://github.com/Wesley-DeMontigny/Smc-CAT)
A C++ sequential Monte Carlo sampler for the CAT Model (an important infinite mixture Bayesian phylogenetic model).

### [Phylogenetic Occupancy Modeling](https://github.com/Wesley-DeMontigny/Phylogenetic-Occupancy-Model)
A statistical model for incomplete genomic presence/absence data written with NumPyro and JAX, allowing for efficient multiple imputant of complete genomic content.
